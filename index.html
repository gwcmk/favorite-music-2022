My Favorite Songs of 2022

Greg Kim
December 28, 2022

It’s been a tough year for me, but I’m glad that I was able to find so much meaning, comfort, and joy in the music I loved. For so many of these songs, I have memories that I think will stick with me for many years to come.

There’s a moment that I don’t think I’ll ever forget while I was at Phoebe Bridgers’ set at Coachella. I was still feeling numb about everything. She was performing the same set that I had seen a few months earlier at the Greek Theatre in Berkeley, and it was impossible not to think about the parallels between then and how I felt in that moment. Despite being surrounded by my friends and so many people, I overwhelmingly felt so alone. Midway through that set, she performed a new song. That was my first time hearing Sidelines, and looking back now, hearing her sing the chorus was the first time that I really started to consider how much love has changed my life.

Watch the world from the sidelines
Had nothing to prove
Till you came into my life
Gave me something to lose
Now I know what it feels like
To wanna go outside
Like the shape of my outline

As I’ve reflected about the past few years, I’ve thought a lot about what has changed since the lowest points in my life, and what Phoebe is singing about here is undoubtedly the biggest reason for the changes since then. There’s a quote I like from Thomas Merton in All About Love about the transformative power of love: “Genuine love is a personal revolution. Love takes your ideas, your desires, and your actions and welds them together in one experience and one living reality which is a new you.” Thinking about this gives me so much hope for the future. I’ll keep hanging on to that.
